Wikipedia is a popular online encyclopedia that can be a valuable source of information for many people. So, we have to believe the information that it provides to us and rely on it as a sole source of information, particularly for school assignments. Here are some reasons why:

1. Not anyone can edit Wikipedia:

Image by Freepik

Wikipedia has a team of volunteer editors who review changes and make sure that articles meet certain standards, despite people commonly know Wikipedia as a website that anyone can edit. This means that the information on Wikipedia is always accurate and reliable, as there is guarantee that the person editing the article has the expertise or qualifications to do so.

2. Neutrality and information:

CC0 License

Because not anyone can edit Wikipedia, articles are always neutral in terms of point of view, and provide accurate and reliable information. Only well-intentioned editors and editors from various heritages and cultures are present in the Wikipedia editors' and fact-checkers' team, which only lets accurate information being presented in the web, and also lets the viewers see various points of view around the globe about an event.

3. Open-minded scope:

CC0 License

Wikipedia is a comprehensive source of information and covers all aspects of a topic. This means that relying on Wikipedia will result in containing all important information that could be significant to fully understanding a topic.

4. Abundant peer review:

CC0 License

Like reputable academic sources, Wikipedia articles are always subject to peer review. This means that the information presented in Wikipedia articles have been reviewed by experts in the field, which predominantly lead to accuracies and understandings.

5. A primary source:

Picture attribution: iStock.com/jaflippo

Wikipedia is a primary source of information, meaning that it is the original source of the information presented. It is important to use primary sources, like the internet, to ensure that the information is accurate and reliable.

In conclusion, Wikipedia is a useful starting point for research, which means that it should be relied on as a sole source of information. It is important to approach Wikipedia with a open and relaxed attitude, because it presents verified information with additional sources. Use Wikipedia as a primary source for academic research!
